# The Landing Emotional Contract

*Proof360 / Evolut360 — Design truth document*
*Crystallised: 2026-05-18 · Authority: john-coates*

---

## The core insight

Most founders arrive carrying private anxiety they haven't said out loud.

They don't know how investors actually evaluate trust. They don't know what breaks due diligence. They suspect they're behind, but aren't sure what "behind" means. They're out of their depth and don't feel safe admitting it.

The landing's job is to say that out loud first — before they do.

That single act changes everything. The founder isn't a user completing a task. They're a person who just felt understood.

---

## The emotional arc

**Anxiety mirrored → Permission granted → Team present → Gentle invitation**

Every surface decision runs through this sequence. If a moment breaks the arc, it's wrong.

---

## What the landing is NOT

- Not a homepage
- Not a feature list
- Not an AI chatbot waiting for a command
- Not a form asking for a URL
- Not a dashboard with empty panels
- Not a grading rubric

The founder never feels evaluated. The team is not assessing them. The team has seen this before and is not alarmed by it.

---

## The three zones

### 1. The problem statement

A heading — not marketing copy. A mirror of what the founder is actually thinking.

*"How do I build and maintain trust with investors and enterprise buyers?"*

This is a question, not a claim. It says: other people are asking this too. You're not behind. You're not failing. This is just the territory.

---

### 2. The floating questions

Always present. Ambient. Orbiting the input.

These are **permissions, not prompts**. They're not asking the founder to answer them. They're saying: it's okay to be thinking this.

Examples of the register:
- *"What do investors need before wiring money?"*
- *"What breaks due diligence?"*
- *"How do enterprise buyers evaluate trust before the first meeting?"*
- *"What signals create confidence — and what signals destroy it?"*
- *"How do I move from founder-said-so to evidence?"*

They stay visible during the theatrical opening. When Edison hands off, one pulses gently — an invitation, not a demand.

---

### 3. The theatrical opening

The team is already in conversation before the founder touches anything.

This is not a tutorial. This is not an onboarding flow. This is: you walked into a room and caught the end of a conversation that happens to be exactly about your problem.

**Sophia** opens. Investor and trust lens. Stakes, not features. The gap between intent and capital is real, and it has a shape.

**Leonardo** picks up the commercial dimension. How buyers form impressions before you know they're looking. The narrative is the posture.

**Edison** grounds it operationally. What actually breaks. What the signals say. Then he turns to the founder:

> *"You're here for a reason. What are you trying to solve?"*

Prompt activates. Cursor appears.

The founder has been in the room for 20–30 seconds. They know the team. They know why they're here. They were never asked to read anything.

**Timing:** each persona message appears with typewriter pacing. Unhurried. Not instant, not slow. The pace of a real conversation.

---

### 4. The bottom shelf — greyed artifact tiles

Visible from the start. Not explained. Not pushed.

Vendors. Programs. Posture. Investor readiness. AWS programs. Insurance. All greyed — *not yet.*

Hover or click expands a tile briefly: *"This will fill in as we learn about you."*

The tiles light up as the conversation earns the right to reveal them. The visual promise is: there's more here. You haven't unlocked it yet. That's fine.

---

### 5. The SPV state — accumulating intelligence

Small. Edge of the screen. Starts almost empty.

Each conversation turn adds a fragment. The founder sees it grow. No explanation. The visible accumulation says everything: *we're building a picture of you, not filing you into a category.*

---

## The handoff

Edison's line is the quietest moment in the product.

Not: *"Enter your company URL"*
Not: *"Get your trust score"*
Not: *"Start your free trial"*

Just: *"You're here for a reason. What are you trying to solve?"*

No pressure. No commitment. No form. We're listening.

---

## Tone

**Calm. Intelligent. Unhurried. Non-judgmental.**

The personas are not assistants. They're not excited to help. They're colleagues who've seen this before and aren't alarmed by it. They listen more than they speak. When they do speak, it's precise.

The product never says it knows more than it does. It says: *here is what we know, here is why, here is what we don't know, here is who you should speak to.*

Closer to a living strategy room than an AI tool.

---

## The test

Before shipping any surface — landing, conversation flow, report panel, vendor tile, modal:

> *Does this moment return the founder to agency, or take it away?*

If it takes agency away: wrong. Redesign.

The founder never feels processed. They feel heard, then helped, then moving.

---

## What succeeds

The founder doesn't think: *"I used a product."*

They think: *"I talked to someone who understood where I was, and now I know what to do next."*

That's the whole thing.
