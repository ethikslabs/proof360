## The Context

Proof360 exists today as a working product: three-surface architecture (marketing site, scored report, partner portal with lead routing), built via Claude Code over recent months, grew from a single-page site to a multi-surface product. It carries real commercial momentum — AWS event contacts, Ingram senior sales contact requesting an MD introduction, Cisco-connected individual, conversations routing toward Marketplace strategy.

The current code pre-dates the pentad. It was built before VERITAS went live, before VECTOR routed inference, before PULSUS existed, before IMPERIUM and NEXUS were conceived. It implements — or doesn't — its own versions of what the pentad now provides as governed primitives.

This convergence decides what proof360 *becomes* when it is rebuilt as a native module on the pentad substrate.

## What Proof360 Is (The Product Thesis)

Proof360 is the trust readiness assessment product. It discovers gaps in a company's trust posture (compliance, security, governance, customer trust signals) and produces a scored report with vendor recommendations. The commercial structure is relationship-driven: vendors (Vanta, Cisco, Palo Alto, Cloudflare, Okta/Auth0, Fireblocks, Frankieone, Cognitive View, Cyber Pro Insurance, Ingram Micro, Dicker Data) receive lead routing when proof360 identifies a gap they can fill.

The positioning is "Transparent Palantir" — vendor-agnostic, trust-first, day-zero provisioning framing. The value proposition: a company sees its own trust posture through an honest lens and gets routed to the right vendors to close gaps.

**One-line:**
> Proof360 shows a company where they are on trust readiness and connects them to the vendors who can close the gaps. The commercial engine is the vendor routing layer.

## What Proof360 Becomes on the Pentad

Not a standalone product. A **module on the pentad substrate** that consumes every plane:

- **VECTOR** — all inference (report generation, chat, persona conversations) routes through VECTOR with sovereignty enforcement and cost tracking
- **VERITAS** — every claim in the scored report is a governed claim. Gap findings become attestable. Vendor recommendations carry provenance.
- **PULSUS** — proof360's spend (inference, AWS, third-party API calls) is metered through PULSUS. Partner portal sees cost per lead generated.
- **IMPERIUM** — proof360 appears in the awareness dashboard alongside other modules. Its state renders through role and persona lenses.
- **NEXUS** — proof360 exposes `module.proof360.*` intents. External callers (partners, integrations) reach proof360 through intent envelopes, not direct API.

Proof360 inherits the pentad's guarantees without reimplementing them: sovereignty, cost truth, provenance, awareness, governed routing.

## What This Convergence Must Resolve

### 1. Surface architecture under the pentad

Current proof360 has three surfaces (marketing, report, partner portal). Does the rebuild preserve three surfaces? Collapse them? Expand them? What does each surface become when pentad-native?

### 2. Report generation pipeline

Current report is generated by an inference pipeline inside proof360. Rebuilt on the pentad:
- Intake → intent envelope to NEXUS
- NEXUS binds to proof360's assessment logic + VECTOR for inference
- Each finding attested to VERITAS as a governed claim
- Report rendering reads attested claims, renders through active persona/role
- PULSUS meters the whole pipeline

What's the clean version of this? Which proof360 logic stays in proof360, what moves to the planes?

### 3. Partner portal and lead routing

The commercial engine is the partner portal with gap-matched lead routing. How does this work on the pentad?
- Vendor identities via Auth0 (Identity & Access schema, queued)
- Role switcher per vendor account
- Leads as NEXUS intents? Or direct API surface?
- Lead attribution, routing logic, payment flow — where does each live?

### 4. Persona bar integration

Sophia is already live on proof360 as a chat persona. When the persona bar becomes a governed primitive (queued convergence), proof360 consumes it rather than implementing its own. What does that integration look like, and what's the migration path for the existing Sophia surface?

### 5. Commercial arrangement surface

Proof360's commercial engine is adaptable — AWS Marketplace listing, Ingram channel enablement, Cisco trust-currency extension, Macquarie sovereign service, direct enterprise licence, SPV vertical. Each is a different arrangement. Does proof360 v2 need to support multiple simultaneous arrangements (multi-tenant with per-arrangement config), or does each arrangement get its own proof360 instance?

### 6. Day-zero provisioning framing

Current positioning includes "day-zero provisioning" — a new company can get a proof360 score on day zero. What does that look like when proof360 is a module on the pentad and new tenants spin up through NEXUS-governed provisioning intents?

### 7. The "Transparent Palantir" positioning

This positioning worked when proof360 was a standalone product. Does it hold when proof360 is a module on a substrate that has its own narrative (sovereign pentad)? Or does the positioning shift to "proof360 is what the pentad produces when pointed at trust readiness"?

### 8. Migration / retirement of current code

What happens to the current proof360 codebase during rebuild?
- Option A: freeze and branch
- Option B: retire entirely, rebuild from scratch on pentad
- Option C: keep current running as marketing/demo front-end while pentad-native builds behind it
- Option D: something else

Demo continuity matters. Ingram MD presentation is in the near-term pipeline. The one-page HTML demo may be enough to front the product during rebuild — or may not.

## Known Design Tensions

### Tension 1: Rebuild scope — full or partial

Full rebuild means proof360 v2 is entirely pentad-native. Longer, higher risk, cleaner architecture.

Partial rebuild means shell stays, internals migrate plane-by-plane. Faster, lower risk, technical debt carried forward.

Convergence must resolve which.

### Tension 2: Commercial engine coupling

Proof360's commercial engine (vendor routing, lead attribution, margin flow) is closely coupled to the product experience today. On the pentad, is the commercial engine a separate module that proof360 consumes, or is it intrinsic to proof360?

### Tension 3: Partner portal as module or plane feature

The partner portal gives vendors dashboards. That's an awareness concern. Does it inherit from IMPERIUM (with vendor as a role scope), or does proof360 build its own vendor-facing awareness?

My instinct: IMPERIUM with role-scoped views. But Identity & Access schema is unconverged, so this may need to defer.

### Tension 4: Persona bar versus Sophia integration

Sophia is already on proof360. When the persona primitive converges, Sophia becomes one persona within a standard bar. What changes for users who know Sophia? Does the UX retain the named persona or absorb it into the general pattern?

### Tension 5: Claims as governed truth

If every gap finding becomes a VERITAS claim, proof360 can no longer make claims faster than the attestation pipeline allows. This is a feature (trust integrity) and a constraint (velocity). How much of the report is claim-governed vs. informational?

### Tension 6: Multi-arrangement support

If proof360 serves AWS Marketplace, Ingram channel, direct enterprise, and sovereign deployments simultaneously, does v2 support all from one codebase with config, or does each get a branded instance?

### Tension 7: Day-zero provisioning primitive

"Day-zero" implies new customers onboard in minutes. On the pentad, provisioning becomes a sequence of NEXUS-governed intents (create tenant, issue identity, configure role scopes, initialise VERITAS tenant corpus, begin PULSUS metering). That sequence needs to execute reliably in minutes, not days. What are the performance and automation requirements?

### Tension 8: The demo imperative

Near-term: Ingram MD presentation, AWS Marketplace conversation, potentially Cisco and others. The rebuild cannot block these conversations. Either the rebuild is fast enough to ship before meetings, the current code is frozen but demoable during rebuild, or the one-page HTML fronts the substrate until v2 lands.

## Hard Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Whatever this convergence produces, these must hold:

1. **Pentad-native.** Every pentad primitive is consumed, not reimplemented.
2. **Sovereign by default.** Proof360 must be deployable in sovereign environments (enterprise, classified-adjacent) without architectural change.
3. **Commercial adaptability preserved.** The arrangement engine remains the strength of the product. No decision may reduce the shapes of arrangement proof360 can support.
4. **Ingram demo continuity.** Rebuild must not break demo-ability during the near-term partner pipeline. A functioning demo surface exists at all times.
5. **HX First.** Every surface (customer, partner, vendor) must return the human to agency. No engagement-maximising patterns, no dark UX.
6. **Ethiks360 discipline.** The commercial engine cannot extract rent in ways that violate the underlying ethical thesis. Boundary Doctrine applies — no silent re-entry of adjacent-system work as in-system truth.
7. **Marjo / $150k forcing function.** Rebuild timeline must not sacrifice near-term revenue path. If a decision lengthens time-to-revenue without commensurate long-term gain, it fails this test.
