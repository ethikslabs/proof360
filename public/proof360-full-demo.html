High DMARC — p=none, domain spoofing unblocked 20 minutes ▾
DMARC is one DNS TXT record. It's currently set to p=none, which means any mail server on the internet can send email that appears to come from @acmecorp.io. Change it to p=quarantine (sends suspicious mail to spam) or p=reject (blocks it entirely). This is a 20-minute change, not a project.
This is the single highest-ROI security change you can make today. It directly blocks the phishing attack vector most commonly used against B2B SaaS customers — impersonating your domain to your own customers.
